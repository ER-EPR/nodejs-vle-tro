The Journey of Recovery

1974

Discovery of the Problem

Scientists Sherwood Rowland and Mario Molina discover that chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) are destroying the ozone layer.

1985

Antarctic Ozone Hole

British Antarctic Survey scientists report massive ozone depletion over Antarctica, confirming the crisis.

1987

Montreal Protocol

Nearly every nation on Earth signs the Montreal Protocol, agreeing to phase out ozone-depleting substances.

1990s-2000s

Global Implementation

Countries worldwide replace CFCs with safer alternatives in refrigeration, air conditioning, and manufacturing.

2018

First Signs of Healing

UN reports confirm the ozone layer is healing, with projections for full recovery by mid-century.

2020

Continued Progress

NASA confirms the ozone hole is the smallest it has been since its discovery, measuring just half the size of recent years.
